About Me

I am Bo Li, a research fellow at the Center of Reproductive Sciences, Cincinnati Children’s Hospital. I hold a Ph.D. in Animal Reproduction and Development Biology from South China Agricultural University. My research focuses on investigating the roles of Erbb3 in mouse pregnancy. During my Ph.D., I specialized in elucidating the intricate processes of embryo implantation and decidualization within uterine biology. Currently, I am enhancing my skills and knowledge in uterine biology under the guidance of Drs. S.K. Dey and Xiaofei Sun.

Research

My research is centered on the role of ERBB3 in uterine gland growth and maturation in genetically mutant mice. I have expertise in handling genetically modified cells and animal models, particularly in the context of uterine biology. My ongoing projects include:

  • The role of ERBB3 in uterine gland growth and maturation in genetically mutant mice.
